# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is the life cycle of a moth?

Moths undergo complete metamorphosis: females lay eggs that hatch into caterpillars, which feed and grow rapidly while molting. Before transforming into an adult, the caterpillar molts to form a pupa, often covered in a protective silk cocoon.

## What are the differences between moths and butterflies?

Butterflies have thin, hair-like antennae with a small knob at the end, while moth antennae are varied in appearance — often feathery or thread-like. Butterflies are typically brighter-colored and active during the day; moths are generally drab and nocturnal.

## Are all moths nocturnal?

No. Most moths fly at night, but some are diurnal. Diurnal moths tend to be more brightly colored and can resemble butterflies, though they lack the knob at the tip of each antenna.

## Do all moths spin cocoons?

No. Many species wrap their pupa in silk, but some form a naked or exposed pupa without any cocoon.

## What is the largest moth in the Pacific Northwest?

The giant silk moths (family Saturniidae) are the largest. The Polyphemus Moth can have a wingspan of up to 6.5 inches (16.5 cm). Sphinx moths (Sphingidae) are also substantial, with some reaching 5.5 inches.

## How long do moths live?

It varies by species. Adult moths may live anywhere from a few days to about a month. The timing of generations depends on environmental conditions and the species.

## What are the most economically damaging moths in the region?

Pest species include the codling moth (affects apples), western tent caterpillar moth, and several crop pests such as loopers, cutworms, and armyworms. The Douglas-fir Tussock Moth is a notable forest pest.

## What do caterpillars and adult moths eat?

Almost all caterpillars feed on plants — leaves, stems, roots, or wood depending on the species. Adult moths consume water, plant nectar, rotting fruit, and sap from wounded trees. Many adult moths don't feed at all.

## What roles do moths play in the environment?

Moths are important links in food webs, providing food for insects, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish, and mammals. Their larvae are also significant herbivores and decomposers.

## Are there any threatened or endangered moths in the Pacific Northwest?

Yes. Two species have endangered status in British Columbia: *Anarta edwardsii* and *Copablepharon fuscum*.

## Which moths are endemic to the Pacific Northwest?

Several species are restricted to specific areas within the region, including islands such as Haida Gwaii and the Gulf of Georgia, as well as the Columbia Plateau and various mountain ranges.

## Which moths have the most records in the database?

Over 80 species have 250 or more records. *Malacosoma californicum* leads with around 780 records.
