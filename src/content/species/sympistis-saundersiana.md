## Identification



##### Adults

*Sympistis saundersiana* is a distinctive small (FW length 12 - 13 mm) purplish gray moth with a circular orbicular spot, finely streaked pattern on the mid-wing in the gray terminal area, and a black forewing fringe that has been reported as occurring in British Columbia and flies in September. The median area of the forewing is smooth purplish gray with a nearly straight smoky black median line. The terminal area and fringe are black. The rest of the wing is powdery light purplish gray. The basal, antemedial, and postmedial lines are dark purplish gray, double filled with brown-gray, and smooth. The antemedial line is excurved. The postmedial line is very smooth without scalloping, S-shaped with excurved anterior segment and concave basad lower segment. The subterminal line is strongly wavy on the mid-wing and nearly straight near the costal and trailing margins. The mid-segment is preceded medially by black thin arrowhead-shaped marks and followed laterally by thin black lines between the veins. The adjacent veins are dark. The spots are outlined in dark purplish gray, usually with lighter gray inner lines, and are filled with the ground color. The orbicular spot is round, appearing as a small pale circle. The reniform spot is kidney-shaped, usually less conspicuous than the orbicular spot. The claviform spot is small and lacks pale filling. The hindwing is tan to dark gray with dark veins, postmedial line, and marginal band. The fringe is tricolored, yellow, gray, and white. The head and thorax are smooth very dark brown, with a weak tuft of black scales on the posterior thorax. The male antenna is filiform.

This species can be identified by its small size, banded purplish gray forewing with crisp smooth lines and spots, pattern of fine "squiggles" on the mid-wing near the outer margin, and black fringe.

## Habitat

This species is widely distributed throughout eastern North America, extending westward to the Rocky Mountains in open meadow and prairie habitats. It may prefer habitats with sandy soil and alvars.



## Distribution



### Pacific
 Northwest

Forbes (1954) states that this species has been reported from British Columbia. We have not examined any specimens from our region and its occurrence in the province needs verification.



### Global

*Sympistis saundersiana* is predominantly found in the East. The range extends from southern Canada to New Jersey, southern Ohio, and Wisconsin. More southerly records are known from South Carolina and the Florida Panhandle. The western limit of the contiguous distribution given by Forbes (1954) is Manitoba.



## Life
History



##### Larvae

This species is a foodplant specialist feeding on penstemons (*Penstemon* spp.) in the Scrophulariaceae.



##### Adults

The reported flight period of this moth is September. It is nocturnal and comes to lights.



## Economic Importance

None.
