The American Lappet Moth (*Phyllodesma americana*) is a medium-sized moth in the family Lasiocampidae with a wingspan of approximately 32–42 mm. Adults are cryptically patterned in grey, brown, and tan, with scalloped hindwing margins and a distinctive resting posture in which the wings are held flat and the forelegs are extended forward, resembling a dead leaf or curled bark.

Adults fly from May through July across the Pacific Northwest and are occasionally attracted to lights at night. Larvae feed on the foliage of deciduous trees and shrubs, including alder (*Alnus*), birch (*Betula*), willow (*Salix*), and cherry (*Prunus*). The species overwinters as an egg. It is found in wooded habitats from lowland riparian corridors to mid-elevation forests throughout Washington, Oregon, and Idaho.
