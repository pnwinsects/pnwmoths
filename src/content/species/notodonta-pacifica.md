*Notodonta pacifica* is an attractive warm brown and gray prominent moth with pale hindwings, measuring 21–24 mm. Key features include elongate forewings with a black flange on the inner posterior margin, ochre and red-brown coloration along the posterior wing, and hoary gray tones in the median area. The reniform spot appears as a transverse dark gray or red-brown line surrounded by paler scales. Hindwings are predominantly white with gray suffusion, particularly in females.

The species distinguishes itself through the combination of gray and red-brown forewings with a light gray anterior median area and posterior margin flange. It differs from the similar *Notodonta ochreata* in having a smoother forewing with more extensive brown color on the distal wing, whereas *N. ochreata* displays strong charcoal mottling. Male antennae are bipectinate.

## Habitat

The species typically inhabits mixed hardwood-conifer forests or riparian areas along creeks at lower elevations both west and east of the Cascades, though it is generally rare and sporadic.

## Distribution

Range extends from southern British Columbia's northern Okanagan region southward, with scattered western records from the northeastern Olympic Peninsula and northern Willamette Valley. Globally, *Notodonta pacifica* is confined to the American West, extending through the northern portions of California and Nevada.

## Life History

No specific foodplant records exist, though the species may specialize on aspens based on related *Notodonta* species' preferences. The moth appears single-brooded, with adults present from May through August, with most records before mid-July. Adults are nocturnal and attracted to light.
