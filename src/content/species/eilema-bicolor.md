## Identification

##### Adults

*Eilema bicolor*, the bicolored moth, is a small to medium-sized (FW length 13–17 mm), aptly named, gray and yellow moth that occurs in most of British Columbia and adjacent states east of north-eastern Washington during the summer. Most of the moth, including head thorax, and most of both wings and abdomen are dull medium to dark gray.  The collar, tip of the abdomen, and costa of the forewing are contrasting bright ochre yellow. The forewing is relatively long.

This moth is unlikely to be confused with other species in our area.

## Habitat

This is a northern species that is widely distributed across Canada, primarily in boreal conifer forests.

## Distribution

### Pacific Northwest

The distribution of *E. bicolor* is limited to British Columbia, extreme north-eastern Washington, and western Montana in the Pacific Northwest. In British Columbia it occurs almost everywhere except near the Pacific Coast and in the southwest part of the province.

### Global

*E. bicolor* is widely distributed across boreal North America from Labrador and Massachusetts to Yukon Territory and British Columbia. It ranges south in the Rocky Mountains to southern Colorado.

## Life History

##### Larvae

This species is a foodplant specialist on tree lichens, and particularly favors lichens growing on conifers such as spruces and firs.

##### Adults

Adults occur in late summer and early fall. They are nocturnal and come to lights.

## Economic Importance

None.
