## Identification

##### Adults

*Dolocucullia poolei* is a medium-sized gray moth (FW length 15–17 mm) with a long pointed forewing that occurs in the western Pacific Northwest as far north as the Olympic Peninsula during late spring and summer. Its [antemedial line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#10) is broad on the [costa](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#35) and has a zigzag course, strongly pointed outward in the [fold](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#58). The [forewing](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#59) is less pointed than in genus *Cucullia*. The ground color is usually slightly powdery medium-gray, uncommonly whitish gray, with variable dark shading near the [posterior](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#104) and [outer margin](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#90)s, typically darkest and most extensively in the [posterior](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#104) [median area](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#77). A dark spot is present in the [posterior](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#104) [subterminal area](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#126) in most specimens, often with a slightly lighter gray line at the [outer margin](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#90) near vein [CuA2](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#39). The [transverse](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#138) lines are dark gray, slightly indistinct. The [basal line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#18) is very short, limited to the [costa](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#35). The [antemedial line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#10) is broad on the [costa](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#35) where it is perpendicular or angled toward the [outer margin](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#90), thinner and strongly zigzagged below the [cubital vein](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#40) to touch the [postmedial line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#106) in the [fold](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#58). The [median line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#79) is only a dark mark on the [costa](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#35). The [postmedial line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#106) is evenly scalloped, strongly [curved](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#52) around the [cell](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#25), then oblique to the wing to end on the mid-[trailing margin](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#137). The [subterminal line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#127) is a dark mark near the [costa](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#35). The [terminal line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#133) is a series of black spots between the veins, and the [fringe](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#60) is the ground color. The typical noctuid forewing spots are usually absent, although the [reniform spot](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#113) is evident as a dark smudge in some specimens. The [hindwing](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#66) is light gray with a slightly darker brown-gray [marginal band](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#75) and dark gray veins. The [hindwing](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#66) [fringe](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#60) has a pale whitish edge and a light gray base. The head and [thorax](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#134) are the same as the forewing, with a dark V-shaped mark (apex [anterior](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#11)) on the [median](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#76) [collar](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#30) at the base of a slight midline hump. The male antenna is [filiform](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#56).

*Dolocucullia poolei* was recently recognized as distinct from *Dolocucullia dentilinea*, and these moths are similar in appearance. *Dolocucullia dentilinea* occurs in central Oregon and Idaho in the Pacific Northwest. Since they do not overlap, the species can be identified by locality: *D*. *poolei* west of the Cascades and *D*. *dentilinea* farther east. Compared to *D*. *poolei*, *D*. *dentilinea* tends to be lighter gray with finer, more distinct transverse lines. Its antemedial and postmedial lines are angled toward each other without touching and the dark spot near the anal angle is usually absent.

*Dolocucullia poolei* can usually be distinguished from western Pacific Coast moths in other genera by its gray color and the touching zigzag [antemedial and postmedial ](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#10)lines. In this region it resembles *Cucullia intermedia* or *Cucullia florea*, but both of them lack the [antemedial line](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#10). *Dolocucullia poolei *somewhat resembles *Lacinipolia patalis*, a very common moth in western Oregon and Washington during late spring. Compared to *D. poolei*, *L. patalis* has a shorter less pointed [forewing](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#59) with better defined markings that include a black [basal dash](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#17) and a reniform spot. It lacks the dark gray spot near the [anal angle](http://pnwmoths.biol.wwu.edu/about-moths/glossary/#4) of *D. poolei*. Under magnification, the eyes of *Dolocucullia* are smooth and those of *Lacinipolia* are covered by short hairs.

##### Larvae

Unknown.

## Habitat

*Dolocucullia poolei* is found in a variety of habitats. In the Pacific Northwest this species is often common in moist meadows of coastal rainforests within the Coast Range.  It is particularly abundant in disturbed, open clear-cuts of the Oregon Coast Range.  It also occurs less commonly in meadows of the Cascades.

## Distribution

### Pacific Northwest

*Dolocucullia poolei* occurs in western Oregon and Washington in the Pacific Northwest. It is most commonly encountered near the Pacific Coast—and is limited to the immediate coast at the northern end of its range on the Olympic Peninsula—but is found inland as far as the Cascade Crest in parts of Oregon.

### Global

The range of this species extends south in western California to near the border with Mexico. An isolated population is in the high Sierra Nevada range.

## Life History

##### Larvae

No specific information is known regarding larval foodplants of this species, but it probably feeds on herbaceous Asteraceae such as goldenrods (*Solidago* spp.) or gumweed (*Grindelia* spp.). The exotic tansy ragwort (*Senecia jacobaeae*) might be a foodplant in the Oregon Coast Range.

##### Adults

The flight period is late spring and summer in the Pacific Northwest, with records from late May to August. It is nocturnal and comes to light.
